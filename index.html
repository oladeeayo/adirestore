Our Heritage

Woven in Tradition.
Worn with Pride.

The Adire Store Limited was born from a deep reverence for the ancient Nigerian art of adire — the hand-dyed indigo fabric that has dressed Yoruba royalty, market women, and modern tastemakers alike. Each piece in our store is crafted by skilled artisans using traditional cassava-paste resist and natural indigo dye, preserving a living heritage while making it accessible to the world.

How We Create

The Art Behind
Every Thread.

Each piece undergoes a meticulous multi-day process passed down through generations of Yoruba artisans.

01

Design & Pattern

Our artisans sketch traditional Adire motifs — from the iconic Olokun waves to geometric eleko patterns — drawing from centuries of Yoruba visual language.

02

Resist Application

Cassava starch paste or raffia stitching is applied by hand to create resist patterns on premium white cotton, defining where the indigo cannot penetrate.

03

Indigo Dyeing

The fabric is submerged in natural indigo vats multiple times to build depth of colour — a process that can take three to five days to achieve the signature Adire hue.

04

Finish & Style

Each garment is washed, sun-dried, and expertly tailored. Our quality team inspects every piece before it bears The Adire Store name and ships to you.
